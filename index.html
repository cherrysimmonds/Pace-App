Full Recipes with Ingredients & Methods

All recipes serve 2–4 people (Cherry feeds 3, Kerry feeds 4 — just scale up slightly for Kerry). Designed to be made quickly on weeknights.

🍛 Black Bean & Sweet Potato Curry 🍒 Cherry · Serves 3–4 · 25 mins

Ingredients
  • 2 × 400g tins black beans, drained
  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled & cubed
  • 1 × 400ml tin coconut milk
  • 2 tbsp curry paste (tikka or korma)
  • 2 large handfuls baby spinach
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 1 tbsp olive oil · Salt & pepper
  • Brown rice or naan to serve
Method
  1. Fry onion & garlic in oil, 3 mins
  2. Add curry paste, stir 1 min
  3. Add sweet potato & coconut milk
  4. Simmer 15 mins until potato is soft
  5. Stir in black beans, heat through
  6. Wilt in spinach, season & serve
💡 Batch cook double — freezes brilliantly for 3 months.

🍝 Lentil Bolognese 🍒 Cherry · Serves 3–4 · 30 mins

Ingredients
  • 200g dried red lentils (rinsed)
  • 2 × 400g tins chopped tomatoes
  • 1 large onion, finely diced
  • 2 carrots, grated
  • 3 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 2 tbsp tomato purée
  • 400ml veg stock
  • 1 tsp dried oregano, 1 tsp basil
  • Wholegrain spaghetti to serve
  • Parmesan or veggie alternative
Method
  1. Soften onion, garlic & carrot in oil, 5 mins
  2. Add purée, herbs, stir 1 min
  3. Add lentils, tomatoes & stock
  4. Simmer 20 mins, stirring, until thick
  5. Season well. Cook pasta meanwhile
  6. Serve with grated cheese
💡 Make Friday night — perfect carb-load before Saturday's long run!

🥘 Halloumi & Chickpea Tray Bake 🍒 Cherry · Serves 3–4 · 25 mins

Ingredients
  • 250g halloumi, cubed
  • 1 × 400g tin chickpeas, drained
  • 2 courgettes, sliced into half-moons
  • 2 peppers, chopped
  • 250g cherry tomatoes
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp za'atar or mixed herbs
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • Flatbreads or couscous to serve
Method
  1. Preheat oven to 200°C / 180°C fan
  2. Toss all veg & chickpeas in oil & spices
  3. Spread on a large roasting tray
  4. Roast 15 mins, then add halloumi
  5. Roast 10 mins more until golden
  6. Serve straight from the tray!
💡 One tray = one washing up item. The busy parent's best friend.

🌮 Veggie Fajita Feast 🍒 Cherry · Serves 3–4 · 20 mins

Ingredients
  • 2 × 400g tins kidney beans, drained
  • 3 peppers, sliced (mixed colours)
  • 2 large portobello mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 large onion, sliced
  • 2 tsp fajita seasoning
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 6–8 wholegrain tortilla wraps
  • To serve: Greek yogurt, salsa, grated cheese, avocado or guacamole
Method
  1. Heat oil in large frying pan on high
  2. Fry onion & peppers 5 mins until charred
  3. Add mushrooms & beans, season
  4. Sprinkle over fajita spice, toss well
  5. Warm wraps in microwave 30 secs
  6. Build your own — pile on toppings!
💡 Kids build their own wraps = no complaints at dinner!

🍗 Sheet Pan Chicken, Sweet Potato & Broccoli 🌻 Kerry · Serves 4 · 30 mins

Ingredients
  • 6 chicken thighs (skin on or off)
  • 2 sweet potatoes, cubed
  • 1 head broccoli, cut into florets
  • 1 red onion, cut into wedges
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • Salt, pepper & lemon wedges
Method
  1. Preheat oven to 200°C / 180°C fan
  2. Toss veg in oil & spices, spread on tray
  3. Nestle chicken on top, season well
  4. Roast 25–30 mins until chicken is cooked through
  5. Check chicken — juices should run clear
  6. Squeeze lemon over before serving
💡 Double up the veg — leftovers make tomorrow's lunch.

🥘 Turkey & Veg Stir Fry 🌻 Kerry · Serves 4 · 15 mins

Ingredients
  • 500g turkey mince
  • 400g frozen mixed stir-fry veg
  • 3 tbsp low-salt soy sauce or tamari
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 2 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 tsp honey or sweet chilli
  • 2 pouches microwave brown rice
Method
  1. Heat sesame oil in wok on high
  2. Brown turkey mince, 5 mins
  3. Add garlic & ginger, stir 1 min
  4. Tip in frozen veg, cook 3–4 mins
  5. Stir in soy sauce & honey
  6. Microwave rice 2 mins. Serve immediately
💡 Brilliant Friday night carb-load before Saturday long run!

🥘 Egg Fried Rice ✅ Both · Serves 3–4 · 12 mins

Ingredients
  • 3 cups leftover cooked rice (cold!)
  • 4 eggs, beaten
  • 300g frozen peas & sweetcorn
  • 3 spring onions, sliced
  • 3 tbsp low-salt soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tsp ginger paste
  • Kerry adds: prawns or leftover chicken
Method
  1. Heat wok until very hot
  2. Add sesame oil, then cold rice
  3. Stir fry 3 mins until slightly crispy
  4. Push rice to side, scramble eggs in gap
  5. Fold together with veg & soy sauce
  6. Top with spring onions & serve
💡 Cold rice is essential — fresh rice goes mushy. Perfect use for Sunday's leftover rice.

🥚 Batch Egg Muffins ✅ Both · Makes 12 · 25 mins

Ingredients
  • 8 large eggs
  • 100ml milk (any kind)
  • 80g grated cheddar
  • 1 pepper, finely diced
  • 4 spring onions, sliced
  • Handful baby spinach, chopped
  • Salt, pepper, pinch paprika
  • Kerry adds: cooked bacon or ham bits
Method
  1. Preheat oven to 180°C / 160°C fan
  2. Spray 12-hole muffin tin with oil
  3. Divide veg between holes
  4. Beat eggs, milk, cheese & seasoning
  5. Pour egg mix over veg
  6. Bake 18–20 mins until set & golden
💡 Make Sunday evening. Reheat in microwave 45 secs each morning. Keeps 4 days in fridge.

🍌 Runner's Recovery Smoothie ✅ Both · Serves 2 · 3 mins

Ingredients
  • 2 ripe bananas (frozen is best)
  • 2 large handfuls spinach
  • 2 scoops protein powder (vanilla)
  • 400ml oat milk or almond milk
  • 2 tbsp peanut or almond butter
  • 1 tbsp chia seeds
  • Optional: handful frozen berries
  • Optional: 1 tsp honey
Method
  1. Put milk in blender first
  2. Add all other ingredients
  3. Blend on high for 60 seconds
  4. Taste — add honey if needed
  5. Pour & drink within 30 mins of making
💡 Best drunk within 30 mins of finishing a run. The banana replaces glycogen and the protein helps muscles repair.

What Is Autophagy — and Why Should Runners Over 45 Care?

Autophagy literally means "self-eating." It's your body's built-in cellular housekeeping system — a process by which your cells identify and break down damaged, worn-out or dysfunctional components, then recycle them into fresh building blocks for new, healthy cells.

Think of it as your cells running a deep clean. Each cell is a tiny factory that over time accumulates broken machinery and junk. Autophagy is the clean-up crew that removes the debris, refurbishes the equipment, and keeps everything running at peak efficiency. Without it, damaged cellular components pile up — accelerating ageing, inflammation, and disease.

As we age, autophagy naturally slows down. This is one reason why women in their 40s and 50s often experience weight gain, slower recovery and increased inflammation. The good news? Running and strength training are two of the most powerful autophagy triggers available to you — and you're already doing both.

The process earned Yoshinori Ohsumi the Nobel Prize for Medicine in 2016 — which tells you everything you need to know about how significant it is.

🍒🌻

Why This Is Particularly Relevant for Cherry & Kerry

⚖️ Weight loss
Autophagy helps the body burn fat more effectively, and many people report improved sleep quality and reduced inflammation alongside fasting-triggered autophagy. This process supports your 10kg goal without extreme calorie restriction.
🏃 Running performance
Autophagy encourages the disposal of worn-out mitochondria and the creation of newer, more efficient ones — your muscles literally become better at generating energy the more you train. Your endurance improves as a direct result.
🛡️ Anti-ageing & cell renewal
Autophagy is associated with anti-ageing principles and may lead to clearer, more youthful skin and a reduction in menopausal symptoms such as hot flushes. Not bad for something your training is already triggering!
⚡ Recovery & muscle repair
Autophagy is one of the ways the body repairs and rebuilds after training. When this clean-up system is dialled in, you get better repair, more resilient muscle tissue, and a healthier metabolism. Key to sustaining 4 sessions a week.
🧠 Mental clarity
Many women report significantly improved mental clarity and focus during fasting windows. Autophagy in the brain supports mitochondrial function and protects cognitive health. Useful for a COO and busy working mum alike!
🦠 Immune function
Autophagy may positively influence the production of healthy white blood cells and improve markers of inflammation. Ultra marathon training stresses the immune system significantly — autophagy helps buffer this.

8 Practical Tips to Maximise Autophagy

These are realistic, doable strategies for busy women in training — not extreme biohacking. Each one amplifies what your running and strength sessions are already doing.
🌙 Tip 1: Use your overnight fast — it's already working
A 12-hour overnight fast already begins to trigger autophagy. Simply pushing breakfast back by an hour or two on strength days can meaningfully amplify this. Try: finish eating by 7pm, breakfast at 9am = 14-hour fast. Completely manageable for a busy morning. Most studies agree that autophagy meaningfully kicks in after around 16–18 hours, so even modest extensions matter.
☕ Tip 2: Black coffee or plain tea before Monday's strength session
Black coffee and plain green or black tea do not break a fast and may enhance autophagy by activating AMPK — a key autophagy-triggering pathway. Doing Monday's 10-minute strength session before breakfast (with only black coffee) combines fasted exercise with overnight autophagy. Cherry's note: plant milks do contain calories and will break the fasted state — save the oat milk latte for after.
🍽️ Tip 3: Try a 10-hour eating window on rest days
Time-restricted eating — eating within a 10-hour window (e.g. breakfast at 8am, finish eating by 6pm) — leaves a 14-hour overnight fast, which combined with morning exercise is genuinely effective. Important: on long run Saturdays, eat a full breakfast beforehand — do not restrict food before a long effort.
🥗 Tip 4: Reduce refined carbs and sugar between meals
High blood sugar and insulin suppress autophagy. You don't need to go keto — simply keeping snacks to protein and healthy fats keeps insulin lower between meals, creating longer windows of autophagy activity. Every time you choose nuts and Greek yogurt over biscuits, you're creating a better cellular environment. Those Snack Hub swaps? This is another reason they matter.
😴 Tip 5: Protect your sleep — it's your deepest autophagy window
Your overnight fast happens during sleep, and this is when autophagy is most active throughout the whole body, including the brain. Aim for 7–9 hours consistently. Avoid eating within 2 hours of bed — this gives your digestive system time to wind down and autophagy time to ramp up. The 8hrs sleep tick in the Nutrition habit tracker is there for exactly this reason.
🫐 Tip 6: Eat autophagy-supporting foods daily
Certain foods contain compounds that activate autophagy pathways, particularly spermidine (wheat germ, mushrooms, soybeans, aged cheese, peas) and polyphenols (green tea, berries, turmeric, extra virgin olive oil, dark chocolate). These work even outside fasting windows.

💧 Tip 7: Stay hydrated — dehydration suppresses autophagy
Water is essential for the lysosomal process that autophagy relies on. On rest and fasting days, aim for 2–2.5 litres of water plus herbal teas. On run days, increase to 3 litres minimum with electrolytes. Drink a large glass of water first thing in the morning before coffee to support completion of overnight autophagy and rehydrate after the overnight fast. Plain sparkling water is fine — it does not break a fast.
🏃‍♀️ Tip 8: Train in the morning before breakfast where possible
Morning training in a fasted state stacks autophagy signals: you arrive already in mild autophagy from overnight, then exercise deepens it further. Around 30 minutes of moderate-to-high-intensity exercise can trigger autophagy on its own. Monday's strength session and Saturday's long run are your best opportunities. If morning training isn't always possible, don't worry — exercise triggers autophagy regardless of timing. This is optimisation, not obligation.

Important: What Not to Do

🚫 Never fast before a long run or hard effort. You need carbohydrate fuel for runs longer than 45 minutes. Autophagy from long runs comes from duration and effort — not from arriving depleted.
🚫 Don't under-eat to force autophagy. The female hormonal system is sensitive to nutritional stress. Extreme restriction can disrupt hormones and slow metabolism. This is optimisation, not starvation.
🚫 Don't skip post-run protein. Eat protein within 30 minutes of any run or strength session. Muscle repair requires it. Extend your eating window after, not before, training.
🚫 Don't obsess over it. The biggest autophagy gains come from consistent training, good sleep, and not eating late at night. If tracking this adds stress, drop it — stress suppresses autophagy too.
💡 Always consult your GP before making significant changes to your eating patterns, particularly if you take any medication, have any health conditions, or are experiencing menopausal symptoms. Fasting affects women differently to men and individual responses vary.

What Is the Dark Downs?

The Dark Downs is a night trail race by UK Ultra, set in the South Downs National Park — a Dark Sky Reserve since 2016. You start as the sun sets, and you run through the night under the stars. It's atmospheric, magical, and completely unlike road running. That's not a reason to be scared — that's the adventure. 🌟
🗺️ Terrain
95% trail, 5% road. Rolling hills, non-technical South Downs. Runnable for first-timers. Fully marked with reflective signage.
🌙 Night Running
Starts at dusk, runs into darkness. Headtorch required (300 lumens min + backup 200 lumens). Cooler temps = better running!
📍 Base Camp
Plumpton Racecourse, Lewes, BN7 3AL. Looped course — you start and finish at race HQ. Free overnight camping available!
📡 Safety
All runners wear GPS trackers. Download What3Words app. Marshals at regular intervals. Aid stations every few km. You are never alone.
